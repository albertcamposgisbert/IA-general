Reduce K‾\overline{K}K to the set of pairs of natural numbers codifying programs such that each domain and image of the function computed by each of such programs has exactly 111 element, and the union of the domains and images of both programs has exactly 444 elements (roughly, the set of pairs of programs whose domains and images have exactly 111 element, and the union of all such domains and images have exactly 444 elements) in order to prove that such set is not semi-decidable (not recursively enumerable).
